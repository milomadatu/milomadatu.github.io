An Introduction

About the Author

Milo Madolora grew up in Irvine, California, in a Vietnamese-Filipino American family. He is a senior at LMA, Class of 2026, headed to Northeastern University in the fall to study Business Administration — and he spends most of his time behind a camera, in front of a timeline, or somewhere in the middle of a marketing brief.

I was always surrounded by culture growing up. From that inspiration, I wanted to document and share what makes my people special — to capture these moments before they pass.

As I gear up for college and the workforce, I'm honing the toolkit: photography, videography, and design. I'm drawn to projects that turn a classroom, a backyard, or a senior class into something worth keeping.

Looking for a summer job, connecting with people interested in innovation, and building something amazing.

“Put yourself in situations where you're not comfortable.” — LeBron James's favorite saying

Field Notes

Marketing

Notes from the field — a marketing internship at the Vietnamese Film Festival, two virtual enterprise companies, a club I founded at LMA, and freelance branding work.

The Vietnamese Film Festival is a nonprofit organization focused on celebrating Vietnamese storytelling, filmmakers, and culture through annual film screenings, community events, and creative programming. The team works across media, outreach, partnerships, and digital promotion to connect audiences with independent Asian and Vietnamese cinema while expanding cultural representation in film.

As a marketing intern, I worked on digital branding, promotional content, and audience engagement across multiple campaigns tied to the festival's programming and social presence. I collaborated with designers, coordinators, and other interns to create marketing assets, organize content calendars, and help maintain a consistent visual identity online. My work included developing graphics, editing short-form promotional content, and supporting campaign rollouts designed to increase awareness and engagement before festival events.

Featured Build · 2026

The Class of 2026 Website

milomadatu.github.io/lma-class-of-2026 ↗

A digital archive of the LMA senior class — 101 biographies, headshots, and class statistics, organized into one browsable site.

Information Architecture Editorial Direction GitHub Pages

Conceived as a living yearbook for LMA, the site is a structured archive built to outlast the printed program. Designed the information model — how a senior, a biography, a photo, and a stat connect — then ran the operation to fill it: tracking down all 101 classmates, collecting every headshot, and shipping the result to GitHub Pages where it lives publicly.
